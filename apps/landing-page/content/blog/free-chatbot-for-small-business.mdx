## What "free" really means: free vs freemium chatbots for small business

Most chatbot platforms offer a free plan, but they all have limits. Here's what you'll typically run into:

- **Truly free:** Essential features with no time limit. Typebot gives you 200 chats/month, full builder access, and AI tools.
- **Freemium:** Basic access, but advanced features and integrations are locked.
- **Free trial:** Full access for a limited time (2-4 weeks), then you pay.
- **Open-source self-hosting:** Install on your own server to bypass vendor fees and limits.

Most free plans also cap conversations (100-200/month), lock certain features, show branding, or limit you to one seat. 

This guide covers tools that are actually useful at $0. **You'll know the limits upfront, so you can build a bot that helps your business — not just one you experiment with.**

## How to build your first free chatbot in 30 minutes

### Step 1: Define your goal and map top questions

A useful chatbot tackles bottlenecks like repetitive FAQs or slow lead capture. **Before you start building, choose one main objective.**

Maybe you want to [qualify leads](./lead-qualification-tools.mdx), handle support, or manage bookings.

Take a look at your recent emails and DMs. Jot down the most common questions you get, aim for about 5 to 10. These will shape your conversation flow.

Think of questions like, “What are your rates?” or “How do I book?” **These are the questions you answer again and again.**

### Step 2: Build your conversation flow

Open Typebot to create your new chatbot.

The visual editor makes it easy to put things together. Just drag and drop blocks to build your chat experience.

<Image src="/blog-assets/typebot-chatbot-builder-v2.avif" alt="Typebot Chatbot Builder V2" width={1200} height={749}  />


- **Welcome message:** Start with a friendly greeting. Let people know what to expect.
- **Input blocks:** Use **Buttons** for quick choices, **Text input** for details, and **Email** or **Phone** blocks to collect contact info.
- **Logic:** With **Condition** blocks, you can send users down different paths. For instance, you might send prospects to your calendar link, while general questions get an FAQ answer right away.
- **Personalization:** Make it feel more human by using variables like "\{\{name\}\}" in your responses. For example, “Thanks, \{\{name\}\}!”
- **Integrations:** Drop in a **Google Sheets** block if you want leads logged automatically, or use a **Webhook** to push info to your CRM.

Wrap up with a clear “next steps” message, so folks know they’re done. **A simple ending makes the chatbot feel complete and easy to use.**

### Step 3: Deploy and test

<Image src="/blog-assets/chatbot-faq-example/share-test-publish-buttons.avif" alt="share test publish buttons" width={831} height={731}  />


Head to the “Share” tab on Typebot and pick how you want to deploy your bot:

- **Bubble:** That classic chat icon in your site’s corner.
- **Popup:** A widget that shows up after someone scrolls or hangs around.
- **Embed:** A full-window chat for special landing pages.
- **WhatsApp:** If your visitors are mostly on mobile.

Before you go live, run through every route in the editor and make sure everything works.

Also check it on your phone to confirm the layout looks good.

If all’s well, grab the embed code and install it in your website’s header or body. **Testing both the flow and the design helps prevent broken experiences.**

Getting a lead-generation bot live won’t take long.

By sticking to the questions you hear most often and automating that data entry with integrations, you save yourself manual work. You also speed up responses for your prospects.

## Key metrics to track for your free chatbot

If you’re trying to figure out whether your chatbot is actually useful, it’s worth looking past just how many people use it.

**These metrics show whether visitors are engaging, converting, and saving your team time.**

### Engagement metrics

- **Chat started rate:** This is the percentage of visitors who decide to start a conversation with your bot. If this number’s low, it could mean your welcome message needs tweaking or that your widget isn’t standing out on your site.
- **Completion rate:** Out of the people who start chatting, how many finish what you intended, like submitting their info or getting an answer? This tells you if your script is clear and moving at a comfortable pace.
- **Drop-off points:** Where do users exit the conversation? If people tend to leave at a specific question, it’s probably time to rethink how you ask it, or even consider adding a human contact option.

### Business impact metrics

- **Leads captured:** This counts the unique users who give you their contact info or take a conversion action, like booking an appointment. It’s a direct link between your bot and your bottom line.
- **Time savings:** Estimate how many hours you save by automating routine tasks, such as checking order status or answering FAQs. For smaller teams, this can mean saving several hours of work every week.

### Iteration through analytics

Most chatbot platforms, like Typebot, track these stats for you.

**The built-in analytics make it easy to see, step by step, where visitors complete or drop off.**

<Image src="/blog-assets/chatbot-for-real-estate-agents/typebot-example-analytics-flow-chart.avif" alt="Typebot Example Analytics Flow Chart" width={1200} height={818}  />


Right in the visual builder, you can also export conversation histories as CSV files if you want to dig into lead quality or compare performance across different channels.

By regularly checking these numbers, you create a simple [feedback loop](./how-to-collect-customer-feedback.mdx).

Maybe you shorten a chat flow to bump up completion rates, or you fine-tune instructions wherever people tend to drop off.

**Consistent monitoring turns a basic chatbot into a trustworthy sales and support tool.**

## When free is no longer enough: signs to upgrade

Free plans work well for most small businesses, especially at the beginning.

But as things start to pick up, certain situations signal it is time to consider moving to a paid tier or even self-hosting.

### Triggers for upgrading

- **Hitting usage caps:** If you regularly bump up against your 200 monthly chats on Typebot, or hit lower limits on other platforms, the chatbot is holding back your growth. You will miss leads or frustrate users just because you are out of quota.
- **Team expansion:** Most free plans are built for a single admin. As your team grows, you will likely want multiple people managing conversations or digging into analytics, and the free tier will not cut it.
- **Brand presentation:** That **"Powered by" watermark can make your business look less polished.** Upgrading generally means you can white-label and add custom branding, which tends to matter more as you scale.
- **Advanced AI and analytics:** Free versions typically offer basic metrics. Paid plans unlock deeper drop-off analysis and better AI integrations, helpful for handling more complex or open-ended questions.

### Scaling options

On Typebot, the **Starter plan ($39/month)** gets you 2,000 chats and two team seats.

If you need more, the **Pro plan ($89/month)** supports up to 10,000 chats and five seats.

If you have an in-house tech team, Typebot’s **open-source edition** lets you self-host. That way, you remove all chat limits and brand watermarks at no cost.

**Catching these milestones early helps you keep your chatbot growing with your business, instead of letting it hold you back.**
