## WhatsApp business API vs WhatsApp Business App

<Image src="/blog-assets/best-whatsapp-ai-chatbot/whatsapp-business-app-vs-api-illustration.avif" alt="whatsapp business app vs api illustration" width={1200} height={822}  />


### WhatsApp Business App

The free WhatsApp Business App is best suited for small businesses that want to handle customer support manually.

Automation here is pretty minimal, think basic away messages and simple [auto-replies](./whatsapp-business-automated-messages.mdx).

<Image src="/blog-assets/best-whatsapp-ai-chatbot/whatsapp-business-application-iphone-screenshots.avif" alt="whatsapp business application iphone screenshots" width={1200} height={803}  />


**You won’t get access to AI-powered chatbots, advanced automated workflows, or a shared inbox for multiple agents.**

### WhatsApp Business API

The API is a different story.

This is Meta’s official solution for companies that need to manage conversations at a larger scale.

You’ll need the API if you want to:

- Deploy AI chatbots and set up automated conversation flows
- Let several team members manage chats from one place
- Connect WhatsApp to your CRM or other marketing tools for smoother, more automated customer journeys

There’s some setup involved.

To get started, you’ll need a Facebook Business account, register your business with Meta, and handle authentication settings in Meta’s Business Manager.

#### Per-message pricing

Unlike the app, the API has a cost — but it's more nuanced than it sounds. As of July 1, 2025, Meta charges per **template message** delivered. Template messages are pre-approved, structured messages that businesses send to users without being prompted — like order confirmations, promo blasts, or OTP codes. Non-template, free-form messages (i.e. replies to a user) are **completely free**.

<Success>
This is actually good news if you're building a chatbot with a tool. Chatbot flows use free-form messages, not templates — so as long as the customer messages you first (opening a 24-hour service window), the entire conversation costs nothing on Meta's side.
</Success>

Where costs do kick in is if you proactively reach out to users with template messages, like promotional campaigns or transactional alerts. Rates vary by country and category, check [our detailed article](./whatsapp-business-api-pricing.mdx).

## How to build a WhatsApp AI chatbot with Typebot

### Step 1: Create your Meta app and get API access

First, **connect Typebot to Meta**. Go to [developers.facebook.com/apps](https://developers.facebook.com/apps), create a new app, choose **Business** as the type, and turn on the WhatsApp product.

<Image src="/blog-assets/create-whatsapp-chatbot/whatsapp-create-app-type.jpg" alt="Meta app creation - selecting 'Other' as the use case" width={827} height={735} caption="Select "Other" for use case, then "Business" as the app type on the next screen" />


<Image src="/blog-assets/create-whatsapp-chatbot/whatsapp-create-app-details.jpg" alt="Meta app creation - entering app name and contact email" width={828} height={442} caption="Fill in your app name and contact email to complete app creation" />


Inside Meta Business Settings, make a System User, give it Admin rights, and add your app as an asset.

<Image src="/blog-assets/create-whatsapp-chatbot/whatsapp-system-user-assets.png" alt="Meta Business Settings - assigning app assets to a system user" width={1200} height={719} caption="Assign your app to the system user with "Manage app" checked to generate a permanent access token" />


Generate an access token. **This token lets Typebot send messages through your WhatsApp number.**

### Step 2: Design your conversation flow in Typebot

Now you can build your conversation in the Typebot editor using its drag-and-drop tools.

<Image src="/blog-assets/typebot-chatbot-builder-v2.avif" alt="Typebot visual drag-and-drop chatbot flow builder" width={1200} height={749} caption="Typebot's drag-and-drop canvas — connect text bubbles, input blocks, condition branches, and AI blocks without writing any code" />


- **Structure:** Add text bubbles and input blocks to collect emails, phone numbers, or button clicks.
- **Logic:** Use condition blocks to split the flow into different responses, and capture user answers with variables for a more personal feel.
- **AI integration:** Add OpenAI, Anthropic, or Groq blocks. Pass the chat history so your AI responses stay in context.
- **Integrations:** Pull live data from your CRM or external APIs with HTTP Request or Webhook blocks.

### Step 3: Configure WhatsApp-specific settings

Tweak your settings so your bot works well on WhatsApp.

<Image src="/blog-assets/whatsapp-automation-chatbot/How-to-add-buttons-to-Typebot.gif" alt="Adding buttons to a Typebot WhatsApp chatbot flow" width={800} height={443} caption="Keep button options to three or fewer per message — Typebot auto-paginates extras, but fewer choices keeps the experience cleaner on WhatsApp" />


- **System variables:** Personalize greetings using the user name and phone number Typebot collects automatically.
- **Session ethics:** Set chats to expire after a period of inactivity, so outdated conversations do not continue where they left off.
- **Button limits:** Keep it to three buttons or fewer per message. Typebot will handle extras, but staying under this limit keeps things tidy.
- **Start conditions:** If you run several bots on the same number, create triggers or use keywords so users get the right bot.
