WhatsApp changes the game completely. Messages achieve 98% open rates with response rates between 45-60%, and the average response time drops to under 5 minutes. Whether you're a SaaS company scheduling demos, an ecommerce brand recovering abandoned carts, or a real estate agent sharing property listings, WhatsApp lets you engage prospects when their intent is strongest. To handle this at scale, automation is essential. Let's explore proven strategies to generate leads on WhatsApp effectively.

### Asking industry-specific qualification questions automatically

A visually appealing chat falls short if it does not separate serious buyers from window shoppers. True lead generation requires progressive qualification. 

The bot strategically places specific input blocks throughout the chat. This extracts the parameters your sales team needs to prioritize leads.

Instead of a long form, the bot asks contextual questions based on the user's industry. Tailor inputs to uncover buying intent with targeted [lead qualification questions](./lead-qualification-questions.mdx) such as:

- **B2B and SaaS platforms:** ask about team size, current software stack, and who else is involved in purchasing decisions.
- **Ecommerce and retail:** use branching logic to assess budgets with questions like what's your budget range for this purchase? Capture urgency by asking about delivery timelines.
- **Professional services:** focus on pain points by asking what's the biggest challenge you're facing right now? Follow up by defining what success looks like for the project.

Each answer saves as a distinct variable. These variables update your CRM or trigger customized responses immediately.

### Integrating WhatsApp leads with your CRM and automating follow-ups

Lead scoring transforms unstructured chat data into a precise, predictive metric. That metric guides your follow-up strategy automatically.

Instead of guessing a prospect's readiness, you calculate it. With Typebot's computation features and variable storage you assign hidden numerical values to specific user behaviors. The conversation unfolds in real time.

The bot updates a score silently in the background. Every interaction builds a continuous intent profile of your prospect.

A reliable framework **categorizes conversation signals into three tiers** using point values. Each tier reflects how strongly the signal indicates a buying decision: 

- **High intent (3 points):** Asking about pricing, requesting a demo, specifying a timeline, inquiring about specific features.
- **Medium intent (2 points):** Engaging with three or more messages, asking comparison questions, downloading resources.
- **Low intent (1 point):** Asking basic questions, giving single-word responses, browsing multiple options aimlessly.

You define these parameters inside your Typebot logic blocks and set actionable thresholds. Prospects with 7 or more points are hot leads flagged for immediate human intervention. 

Users scoring between 4 and 6 are warm leads needing automated educational nurturing. Leads scoring 1 to 3 are cold and should enter a long-term drip sequence to mature without manual sales efforts.

Review and adjust these criteria **monthly based on conversation-to-conversion rates**. This keeps your scoring model accurate and aligned with your buying cycle.

### Pushing qualified contact data directly to your CRM using webhooks

Capturing high-intent data inside WhatsApp is useless if it stays in your messaging app. To keep momentum, you must transfer data instantly to your central system. Webhooks act as the digital nervous system, sending data the moment a user hits a specific milestone in your chat.

Typebot's visual canvas lets you drag a Webhook block directly into your conversation path without writing backend code. When a prospect passes the 7-point lead scoring threshold or completes a qualification form, this HTTP call triggers immediately. 

It maps conversation variables and pushes them straight to your CRM.

For lightweight tracking, you can send data natively to NocoDB, Google Sheets, or Airtable. Enterprise platforms like Salesforce or HubSpot require routing through automation tools. Use Zapier and Make or direct HTTP calls to access any external API.
